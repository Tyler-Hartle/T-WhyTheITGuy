# T-Why the IT Guy — Season 1 Video Scripts

## Episode 1 — The Click Monster

**Topic:** Unknown links and pop-ups  
**Lesson:** Pause before clicking.

**Script:**

T-Why pops onto the screen holding a giant glowing button.

**T-Why:** “Whoa! This button says I won free robot snacks! Should I click it?”

The button wiggles and says: “CLICK FAST!”

**T-Why:** “Hmm. Fast clicks can be tricky clicks. Let’s do the T-Why check.”

On screen:

1. Pause  
2. Ask why  
3. Ask a grown-up

**T-Why:** “If something rushes you, scares you, or promises a prize, stop and ask first.”

**Question:** “What should you do before clicking a prize link?”

**Answer:** “Pause and ask why.”

**Closing:** “Smart clicks keep your tech safe. I’m T-Why, and I’ll see you on the next mission!”

## Episode 2 — Password Pizza

**Topic:** Strong passwords  
**Lesson:** Longer passwords are stronger.

**Script:**

T-Why wears a chef hat and holds a pizza.

**T-Why:** “Today we’re making Password Pizza! A weak password has one topping: dog123. Yuck.”

He adds toppings labeled: uppercase, lowercase, number, symbol, and secret words.

**T-Why:** “A stronger password is longer and harder to guess. Don’t use your name, birthday, pet, or school.”

**Question:** “Which is safer: `cat123` or `PurpleRocket!River77`?”

**Answer:** “The longer one with more ingredients.”

**Closing:** “Make your password like a secret recipe.”

## Episode 3 — Stranger Danger in Game Chat

**Topic:** Online strangers  
**Lesson:** Private info stays private.

**Script:**

T-Why is playing a game. A chat bubble pops up: “What school do you go to?”

**T-Why:** “Uh-oh. That question is asking for private information.”

On screen: address, school, phone number, passwords, real name.

**T-Why:** “Even if someone seems nice, we do not share private info in game chat.”

**Question:** “What should you do if a stranger asks where you live?”

**Answer:** “Stop and ask a grown-up.”

---

## Episode 4 — AI Helper or Trickster?

**Topic:** AI awareness  
**Lesson:** AI can help, but it can be wrong.

**Script:**

T-Why asks an AI robot: “How many moons does Earth have?”

The AI confidently says: “Seventeen!”

**T-Why:** “That sounded confident, but it was wrong. AI can be helpful, but it still needs checking.”

**T-Why:** “Before using AI for school, facts, or big choices, check with a grown-up or a trusted source.”

**Question:** “Is AI always right?”

**Answer:** “No. Helpful does not mean perfect.”

---

## Episode 5 — The Download Dragon

**Topic:** Downloads and apps  
**Lesson:** Unknown downloads can be risky.

**Script:**

A cartoon dragon guards a shiny “free game mod.”

**T-Why:** “Free game mod? Cool. But wait. Where did it come from?”

**T-Why:** “Downloads can carry tricks. Before installing anything, ask a grown-up and check if the source is trusted.”

**Question:** “Should you download a random mod without asking?”

**Answer:** “No. Ask first.”

---

## Episode 6 — The Kindness Keyboard

**Topic:** Online kindness  
**Lesson:** Words matter online.

**Script:**

T-Why types an angry message. The keyboard turns red.

**T-Why:** “Whoa. My keyboard is heating up. Maybe I should pause.”

He rewrites the message kindly.

**T-Why:** “If it would hurt out loud, pause before typing it online.”

**Question:** “What should you do before sending an angry message?”

**Answer:** “Pause and choose better words.”
